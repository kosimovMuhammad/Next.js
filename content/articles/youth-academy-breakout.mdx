Six months ago, he was playing reserve-team football in front of a few hundred
people. On Saturday, he came off the bench in front of fifty-two thousand and
scored the winner within four minutes.

## A fast track through the ranks

Academy graduates rarely skip straight from reserve football to first-team
minutes that matter, but an injury crisis at the club forced the issue. What
started as an emergency call-up has turned into a genuine tactical option —
the manager named him among the substitutes for six straight games and has
now used him in five of them.

## What makes him different

Coaches who worked with him at academy level describe a player who was
technically ready years before he was physically ready. That gap has closed
quickly over the last year, and the composure that made him stand out as a
15-year-old — a willingness to take a touch under pressure rather than panic
— is exactly what's translating at senior level now.

## The next step

Nobody at the club is getting carried away publicly, but privately, there's
real excitement about building a role for him next season rather than treating
these appearances as a stopgap. For a boyhood fan of the club making his name
this way, the story is already better than anything a scriptwriter would have
dared propose.
