Sitting in the corner of an almost-empty training ground cafe, the captain
looks relaxed for someone whose team hasn't lost a competitive game in eleven
weeks. "You'd think it gets easier the more you win," he says. "It doesn't.
Every game just matters slightly more than the last one."

## On the pressure of a title race

"We talk about the table every single day. Not in a stressful way — it's just
information. You look at it, you see what a win does, what a draw does, and
then you forget about it until the next Monday."

## On squad depth deciding the league

Asked what separates this season's group from previous ones that fell short,
he doesn't hesitate: "Numbers twelve through twenty. Two years ago, if three
of our starters got injured in the same month, we were in trouble. This
year, whoever comes in barely changes how we play."

## On the run-in

Six games remain, four of them against sides in the bottom half of the table.
"Everyone will tell you those are the easy ones. Those are actually the
dangerous ones — because you *know* you're supposed to win, and the moment you
start thinking about the result instead of the performance, that's when it
goes wrong."
