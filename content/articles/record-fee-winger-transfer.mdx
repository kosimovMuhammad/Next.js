After three weeks of stop-start negotiations, the deal was finally confirmed
on Saturday morning: a new club-record fee for a 23-year-old winger who spent
last season terrorizing defenses in Serie A.

## Why this fee makes sense

Record fees invite skepticism, and rightly so — recent history is littered
with expensive signings who never adapted. But the profile here is different.
He's young enough to still be improving, already elite in the metric that
matters most for wide players (progressive carries into the box), and has
publicly wanted this move for over a year.

## The tactical fit

The incoming manager plays with inverted wingers who tuck inside to overload
the half-spaces. That's exactly where this signing did his best work last
season, cutting in from the right to shoot or thread passes into onrushing
fullbacks.

## What's next

A medical is scheduled for Monday, with an unveiling expected before the end
of the week. Attention now turns to whether the club will reinvest more of
this summer's transfer budget, or consider the window largely complete with
one marquee arrival.
